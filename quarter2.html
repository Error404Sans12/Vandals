Streaming Platform That We Will Use

OBS Studio


OBS Studio is a well-known open-source program for recording gameplay and, more especially, broadcasting it to a global audience. You may stream video games with OBS Studio to websites like Twitch and YouTube. OBS is also used to record screencasts. Although it's a strong program, it's certainly overkill for anyone looking for a simple and quick game capture option. If you're prepared to invest the time to understand it, OBS Studio has a ton of settings and functions, and it gives incredible versatility for power users. When you first launch OBS Studio, a wizard will assist you in setting up your system for streaming or recording video, reducing the learning curve. OBS functions best in a multiscreen configuration where you can play your game on one screen while watching OBS on another. However, this is not necessary. OBS doesn't display an overlay inside the game, in contrast to the other programs. It merely works in the background instead. Additionally, you must instruct OBS to begin streaming or recording as soon as your game launches or begins to run. OBS is preferable to other options in several circumstances. OBS Studio is a fantastic choice if your goal is to record your gameplay and subsequently turn it into a YouTube playthrough guide, for instance. Additionally, you can include a picture-in-picture webcam shot, custom watermarks, and more in your stream.
